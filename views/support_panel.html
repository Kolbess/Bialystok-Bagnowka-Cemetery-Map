Help & Support

How do I find a specific grave? ▼
Use the sidebar filters to search by Section, Row, or Surname. You can also use the Surname Search tab in the top navigation to search our full database of burial records. Click on any section in the map to zoom in and browse individual graves.
What do the map colors mean? ▼
Gold highlights indicate search results or selected graves. The map sections are labeled with their section numbers. Click on the Legend tab in the navigation bar for a full explanation of all map symbols and restoration status indicators.
How can I support the restoration? ▼
The Bialystok Cemetery Restoration Foundation (BCRF) accepts donations to fund ongoing preservation work. When viewing a specific grave's detail page, you'll find a "Support Our Restoration Work" button. You can also visit our main website for more information about volunteer opportunities and sponsorship programs.
What data is available for each grave? ▼
Each located grave includes the person's name, section, row, plot number, and years of birth and death where known. Many graves also have photographs and restoration status notes. Unlocated graves have name records from historical documents but have not yet been physically mapped.
What is the difference between Located and Unlocated graves? ▼
Located graves have been physically identified and mapped to a specific section, row, and plot in the cemetery. Unlocated graves are known from historical burial records but their exact physical position in the cemetery has not yet been confirmed through field surveys.
